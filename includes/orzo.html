Turkey Meatball and Saffron Orzo

Ingredients

Prep

Fridge Pantry Spices
Parm Breadcrumbs Garlic powder
Butter Orzo Red Chili flakes
Turkey Salt
Lemon Pepper
1 Egg Saffron
Better than Bouillon Chicken Paste (if using) Parsley
Oregano
  1. Chop onions and garlic
  2. Shred Parm (60g total)
  3. Weight 200g Orzo
  4. Scale 80g butter (cut in half)
  5. Zest a lemon (2 tsp)
  6. Measure spices for meatballs: 3 tbsp Parsley, 1 tsp garlic powder, 1/2 red pepper flakes, 1 tsp salt, pepper as yo feel
  7. Measure orzo spices: 1/2 tsp pepper flakes - add to garlic, separately 2 tbsp
  8. Measure bread crumbs: 1/2 cup

Meatballs

  • 1 lb ground turkey
  • 40g parmesean cheese (fresh grated)
  • 3 tbsp dried parsley
  • 2 tsp lemon zest
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • Some chicken stock or water + bouillon powder (enough to cover the bottom of the pan)
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes

Orzo

  • 80g butter
  • 1 onion or shallot, diced
  • 2 garlic gloves, diced
  • “Pinch” of Saffron (I use a container in Germany that has .2 grams)
  • 200g orzo
  • 20g Parmesan cheese
  • 1/2 tsp red pepper flakes
  • 475ml chicken stock
  • 2 tbsp oregano

  1. Make meatballs: Put ground turkey, lemon zest, parm, egg, breadcrumbs in a bowl, add salt and pepper, garlic powder and red pepper, then mix well and form meatballs (between 12-20)

  2. Cook the base for the Orzo: Melt half the butter (40g) with a splash of oil on medium heat, then add onions. Cook for 2 minutes or so, until softened, then add garlic and 1/2 tsp pepper flakes and cook for two more minutes.

  3. Start Orzo cook: Add the orzo, saffron and stock and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to med-low and cover. Cook for around 10 minutes (stirring is allowed: keep an eye on it, if it seems too dry or to be sticking lift the lid a stir).

  4. Make meatballs: Heat a pan over medium high heat and then add some oil, and add the meat balls. Let them get some color (well browned) and flip them carefully (using a fork or tongs). Pour in the stock and bring down to a simmer and allow for cooking (at least 5 more minutes)

  5. Finish orzo: When orzo is done, give it a stir, add the remaining cheese, butter and oregano. Season to taste.
